\begin{letter}{The Editors\\Economics Letters}

\opening{Dear Editors,}

We submit ``Same Storm, Different Boats: Generative AI and the Age Gradient in Hiring'' for consideration in Economics Letters.

A widely discussed ``scary chart'' shows stock prices rising while job postings fall, with AI as the suspected driver. We examine this divergence using 4.6 million Swedish job ads from Platsbanken (2020--2026) matched to the DAIOE generative AI exposure index. A timing identification exploits the fact that Sweden's central bank raised rates in April 2022, seven months before ChatGPT launched. The posting decline aligns with the rate hike, not with AI.

However, beneath this aggregate null, AI is reshaping who gets hired. Using full-population employer declarations from Statistics Sweden, we estimate an employer-level difference-in-differences following Brynjolfsson et al.\ (2025). The average post-ChatGPT effect is modest, but an event study reveals an accelerating decline in employment of 22--25 year olds in AI-exposed occupations, reaching 5.5\% by early 2025, while employment of workers over 50 rose by 1.3\%. The age gradient widens over time, with women 22--25 disproportionately affected. An Oaxaca-Blinder-style reweighting decomposition shows that approximately one-third of the gender gap reflects occupational sorting, while two-thirds operates within occupations. Pre-trends are small in magnitude relative to post-period effects, though formally significant in an overpowered panel.

The contribution is the reconciliation: aggregate posting declines reflect macroeconomic tightening, not AI, but AI operates on hiring composition within firms. This distinction matters for policy, which should target entry-level transition pathways rather than macroeconomic stimulus.

The posting analysis uses only public data. The employment analysis uses restricted register data accessible via SCB's MONA platform. The full replication package will be deposited on GitHub upon acceptance.

The manuscript is approximately 2,000 words. No part has been published or is under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission.

\closing{Yours sincerely,}
